🐾 Pet Safety Guide
How to safely apply liquid fertilizer and lawn chemicals when you have a dog.

Liquid fertilizer and your dog

Liquid fertilizers are generally safer than granular ones because there are no pellets for dogs to ingest off the ground. However dogs are low to the ground, lick their paws, and roll in grass — so timing and watering practices matter. In Orlando's heat and humidity, surfaces stay damp longer, extending the time chemicals remain active on grass blades.

Minimum wait times before letting your dog back on the lawn
2–4 hrs
Liquid Fertilizer
Wait until completely dry to the touch. In Orlando humidity, plan for 3–4 hours. If it's overcast or you applied in shade, wait longer.
2–4 hrs
Fungicides
Low acute pet toxicity. Wait until fully dry. For 3336F with water-in, wait for the lawn to re-dry after irrigation.
24–48 hrs
Celsius WG
Herbicides carry higher risk. Minimum 24 hours after drying. A light watering after the dry period then re-drying further reduces surface residue.
48–72 hrs
Insecticides
Bifenthrin and imidacloprid are the highest concern. Wait until dry, then water lightly and let re-dry before dog access. Wipe paws for several days after.
Should you water in liquid fertilizer when you have a dog?
Yes — watering in after the dry period is a smart extra step
For liquid fertilizer, let it dry fully first (2–4 hours). Then a light watering helps move residual nutrients from the grass blades down into the soil where they are far less accessible to your dog's paws and mouth. Your Kelp4Less blend is low-toxicity, but this extra step is worthwhile for paw-licking dogs. For imidacloprid specifically, the 0.5-inch water-in is required for efficacy anyway — wait for the lawn to re-dry after that before allowing dog access.

Signs of possible chemical exposure in dogs
Watch for: excessive paw-licking, drooling, vomiting, diarrhea, lethargy, skin irritation, or unusual behavior after time on a treated lawn. If symptoms appear, rinse paws thoroughly and contact your vet. Have the product name and label ready. Pet Poison Helpline: 1-855-764-7661.
Best day-of strategy when you have a dog
Apply chemicals early on a day when your dog will be inside or away (boarding, daycare, a walk). Apply liquid fertilizer and fungicides together in the morning — by evening the lawn is dry. Schedule insecticide days separately from weed killer days if possible. Never apply Celsius WG and bifenthrin on the same day — the combined 48-hour restriction is your longest window and covers both products if timed together.
Safety Tips & Burn Prevention
Rules for preventing phytotoxicity, resistance buildup, and label overuse on your Orlando St. Augustine lawn.
Never spray a stressed lawn
Drought stress, heat stress, or visible damage means no chemical applications. Water thoroughly for 2–3 days first. Stressed St. Augustine is significantly more vulnerable to phytotoxicity from every product in this plan — especially Celsius WG, Pennant Magnum, and bifenthrin.
3336F residential per-app max is 2 fl oz per 1000 sq ft (5 oz for your lawn)
Higher rates listed on the label are for golf course greens and non-residential turf. At 5 oz per application you get up to 4 applications per year before hitting the 20 oz annual max. This gives you excellent disease coverage throughout the season if you need it.
Bifenthrin: previous estimate of 12.1 oz/yr was nearly double the true limit
Annual max is ~7.5 fl oz for 2500 sq ft based on the 0.4 lb AI/acre cap for 7.9% formulations. Three applications at 2.5 oz each hits the limit. If a mid-season outbreak requires emergency treatment, switch to lambda-cyhalothrin or permethrin to stay within label limits.
Pennant Magnum — water same day, lawn fully established
April only, after the lawn is completely out of dormancy — no tender new growth. Water in within hours. Phytotoxicity shows up 2–3 weeks after the fact, so plan ahead and don't get confused by delayed browning that appears unrelated to application day.
Rotate all three fungicide groups every cycle
Myclobutanil (Group 3) → Azoxystrobin (Group 11) → 3336F (Group 1) → back to Myclobutanil. Never repeat the same group consecutively. Large Patch and Gray Leaf Spot are your primary Orlando threats. Preventative rotation before symptoms is the only reliable strategy.
Azoxy 2SC is standalone only — no tank mixing
The label explicitly advises against mixing Azoxy 2SC with other fungicides, insecticides, herbicides, or fertilizers. Give it its own spray day. All your other chemicals can be combined.
Application order on multi-chemical days
(1) Pre-emergent — water in if required, wait 24–48 hrs before other products. (2) Fungicide + Kelp4Less blend as tank mix (except Azoxy — apply alone). (3) Insecticide + Kelp4Less. (4) Celsius WG — always separate, never mixed. Confirm tank compatibility with a small test jar before full mixing.
